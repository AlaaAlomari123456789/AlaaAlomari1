The Bladder

The urinary bladder is a hollow, stretchy organ in the lower abdomen that stores urine before it leaves your body through your urethra. Conditions that affect your bladder include incontinence, cystitis, and bladder stones. They can make urinating painful, difficult, or uncontrollable.

What is the bladder?

The urinary bladder is a hollow, spherical-shaped organ that holds urine (pee). For most people, it can hold 500-700 mL (about two cups) of pee. Muscles in the bladder contract and sphincter muscles relax to allow urination.

Function: What does the bladder do?

Your bladder holds urine produced by the kidneys. As it fills, nerves signal your brain to use the restroom. Most people urinate about 950-1,900 mL each day.

Anatomy: Where is the bladder located?

The bladder is in the lower abdomen. Ligaments connect it to other organs and the hip bone. Men: between pubic bone and rectum. Women: in front of vagina and uterus.

Parts of the bladder

Dome (apex), Base (fundus), Body, and Neck (connects to urethra).

Bladder Layers

Urothelium (inner lining), Lamina propria (connective tissue), Muscularis propria (muscle layer).

Common bladder issues

  • Bladder cancer
  • Cystitis (UTI)
  • Interstitial cystitis / Bladder pain syndrome
  • Overactive bladder
  • Urinary incontinence
  • Bladder stones

Symptoms

  • Loss of bladder control
  • Pain or burning during urination
  • Cloudy or bloody urine
  • Frequent urges to urinate
  • Strong or foul-smelling urine
  • Pain during sex

Tests for bladder health

  • Urinalysis
  • Urine culture
  • Imaging (Ultrasound, CT, MRI)
  • Cytology
  • Cystoscopy
  • Urodynamic testing
  • Biopsy

Treatments

  • Medications (e.g., antibiotics)
  • Diet changes
  • Physical therapy
  • Behavioral changes
  • Bladder retraining
  • Surgery

Tips for bladder health

Drink water to flush wastes, avoid bladder irritants (alcohol, coffee, carbonated drinks, acidic juices), and consult a healthcare provider about vitamins like C and D.
