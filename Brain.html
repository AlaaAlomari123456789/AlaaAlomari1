The Brain

The brain is a complex organ that controls thought, memory, emotion, touch, motor skills, vision, breathing, temperature, hunger, and every process that regulates the body. Together with the spinal cord, it forms the central nervous system (CNS).

Gray matter consists mainly of neuron cell bodies; white matter consists mainly of axons wrapped in myelin. Gray matter processes and interprets information; white matter transmits it to other parts of the nervous system.

The brain can be divided into three main parts: cerebrum, brainstem, and cerebellum.

Cerebrum

The largest part, controlling movement, thought, reasoning, speech, vision, hearing, and learning.

Brainstem

Connects cerebrum with spinal cord; includes midbrain, pons, medulla; regulates vital functions like breathing, heartbeat, reflexes.

Cerebellum

Coordinates voluntary movements, posture, balance, and equilibrium.

  • Frontal lobe: Personality, decision-making, movement, speech (Broca’s area)
  • Parietal lobe: Spatial awareness, interpreting touch and pain, Wernicke’s area
  • Occipital lobe: Vision
  • Temporal lobe: Short-term memory, speech, musical rhythm, smell recognition

Pituitary Gland

Regulates hormones of other glands; receives signals from hypothalamus.

Hypothalamus

Controls temperature, sleep, hunger, thirst, memory, and emotion.

Amygdala

Regulates emotion, memory, reward system, and stress responses.

Hippocampus

Supports memory, learning, spatial navigation.

Pineal Gland

Secretes melatonin; regulates circadian rhythms.

Ventricles & CSF

Produce cerebrospinal fluid to cushion the brain and spinal cord, remove waste, deliver nutrients.
